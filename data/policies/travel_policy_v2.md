## 1. General Travel Guidelines

All business travel must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager before booking. Employees must use the company's designated travel booking platform unless the destination is not serviced.

Travel should be booked at least 14 days in advance for domestic trips and 21 days in advance for international trips whenever possible. Last-minute bookings require VP-level approval.

## 2. Flight Booking Rules

- **Economy class** is the standard for all domestic flights and international flights under 6 hours.
- **Premium Economy** may be booked for international flights over 6 hours with manager approval.
- **Business class** is permitted for international flights over 8 hours with VP-level approval, or for C-suite executives.
- **First class** is restricted to CXO approval only and must be justified with a business case.

All flights must be booked through the company travel portal. Loyalty program preferences may be specified but the lowest reasonable fare must be selected.

## 3. Per Diem Rates

Per diem rates cover meals and incidental expenses. The following daily rates apply:

### Domestic (India)
- Bangalore: INR 3,500 per day
- Mumbai: INR 4,000 per day
- New Delhi: INR 3,800 per day
- Chennai: INR 3,200 per day
- Hyderabad: INR 3,300 per day

### International
- London, UK: GBP 150 per day
- New York, USA: USD 200 per day
- San Francisco, USA: USD 220 per day
- Singapore: SGD 250 per day
- Tokyo, Japan: JPY 18,000 per day
- Dubai, UAE: AED 700 per day

Per diem is calculated from the day of departure to the day of return, inclusive. Employees must submit expense reports within 30 days of trip completion.

## 4. Hotel Accommodation

- Domestic stays: maximum INR 8,000 per night (metro cities), INR 5,000 per night (non-metro).
- International stays: up to USD 250 per night or local equivalent. Exceptions require VP approval.
- Hotel bookings should be made through the company portal. Direct bookings are reimbursed only if the portal rate exceeds the direct rate by more than 15%.

## 5. Ground Transportation

- Airport transfers: company-approved taxi services or ride-hailing (Uber/Ola) are reimbursable.
- Car rentals require pre-approval and are limited to mid-size vehicles.
- Public transportation is always reimbursable with receipts.
- Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at INR 12 per kilometre (domestic) or local equivalent.

## 6. Expense Reporting

All travel expenses must be submitted within 30 days of trip completion through the expense management system. Required documentation:
- Original receipts for all expenses over INR 500 / USD 10
- Boarding passes for flights
- Hotel invoices
- Conference/event registration receipts

Expenses submitted after 60 days may not be reimbursed. Fraudulent expense claims will result in disciplinary action.

## 7. Safety and Insurance

Company travel insurance covers all employees during approved business trips. Coverage includes:
- Medical expenses up to USD 500,000
- Trip cancellation and interruption
- Lost baggage up to USD 3,000
- Emergency evacuation

Employees traveling to high-risk destinations must complete a security briefing and register with the company's travel security provider.

## 8. Sustainability

The company encourages sustainable travel practices:
- Use video conferencing when possible instead of traveling
- Choose direct flights to reduce carbon emissions
- Use public transportation when feasible
- Offset carbon for long-haul flights (company-sponsored program)
