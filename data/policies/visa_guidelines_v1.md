## 1. Visa Responsibility

It is the employee's responsibility to ensure they have valid travel documents (passport, visa) before traveling. The company HR team will assist with visa application letters and supporting documents.

## 2. Visa Processing Timelines

Employees must apply for visas at least:
- **30 days** before travel for countries with e-visa or visa-on-arrival facilities
- **60 days** before travel for countries requiring embassy appointments (US, UK, Schengen)
- **90 days** before travel for countries with complex visa processes (China, Russia)

## 3. Common Visa Requirements

### India to United Kingdom
- Business visa required for all work-related travel
- Processing time: approximately 15 working days
- Documents needed: invitation letter, travel itinerary, proof of funds, company letter
- Submit application at least 60 days before travel

### India to United States
- B1/B2 visa required
- Processing time: 30+ working days (varies by consulate)
- Interview required at US consulate
- Documents: DS-160 form, photo, company letter, financial documents

### India to Singapore
- Visa-free for Indian passport holders for stays up to 30 days
- Valid passport with at least 6 months validity required

### India to Schengen Area (Europe)
- Schengen visa required
- Processing time: 15-30 working days
- Apply at the embassy of the primary destination country

## 4. Work Permits

If the business trip involves any form of paid work, consulting, or extended stays beyond 90 days, a work permit may be required in addition to a visa. HR must be consulted before any such arrangements.

## 5. Passport Requirements

- All passports must have at least 6 months of validity beyond the planned return date
- At least 2 blank pages should be available for stamps
- The company will reimburse passport renewal fees for business travelers

## 6. Denied Entry Protocol

If an employee is denied entry at a foreign port:
1. Contact the company emergency travel line immediately
2. Document all interactions with immigration authorities
3. Do not sign any documents without consulting the company legal team
4. The company will arrange return travel as needed
